# Ops, HR, and leadership — interview guide

## Role context

This covers Brian (ops/HR) and Chris (managing partner), who handle the operational and business side of RDC: contracts, SOWs, proposals, HR documentation, onboarding, client relationship management, and business development.

Common work patterns:
- Drafting and adapting legal and business documents (SOWs, change orders, proposals, RFP responses)
- Managing HR processes: job descriptions, onboarding materials, policy documentation
- Client communication at the relationship and business level
- Business development: identifying opportunities, crafting pitches
- Internal communication: team announcements, policy updates, process documentation

## Likely friction areas to probe

- **SOW and proposal generation**: Building client-facing business documents from templates — high repetition, high stakes
- **HR document generation**: Job descriptions, onboarding docs, policy write-ups that follow predictable structures
- **RFP research and response**: Finding relevant RFPs and drafting competitive responses
- **Client communication**: Drafting business-level correspondence that's professionally calibrated
- **Contract or document review**: Reading through contracts to extract key terms, obligations, or flags

## Question sequence

**Q3 — Work focus** (buttons):
> "Where does most of your working time go?"

Options: Business documents — proposals, SOWs, contracts / Client relationships and communication / HR and operations — hiring, onboarding, policy / Internal process and team management / Something else

**Q4 — Document generation** (buttons):
> "When you need to build a business document — a proposal, SOW, change order — what's the most time-consuming part?"

Options: Adapting the template to fit the specific project / Getting the right level of detail in the scope and deliverables / Making sure the language is professional and tight / Going back and forth on wording and specifics / Something else

**Q5 — Open-text moment** (see Step 4 in SKILL.md)

**Q6 — Communication volume** (buttons):
> "How much of your day involves writing — emails, proposals, internal docs?"

Options: It's most of what I do / A significant chunk — maybe half / It comes and goes / Less than you'd think

**Q7 — AI experience** (buttons):
> "Have you used Claude or any AI tools for work before?"

Options: Yes, fairly regularly / A few times, with mixed results / Once or twice but it didn't really stick / Not really

**Q8 — Barrier check** (buttons):
> "What would make an AI solution feel too risky or too complicated?"

Options: Concern about confidentiality — what goes into the AI / If the output needs so much editing it's not worth it / If it only handles simple tasks and misses the nuance in legal/business docs / If it requires significant setup time / Something else

## Output guidance

Strong recommendation candidates for this role:
- An SOW draft generator: paste project details from the brief or email thread, get a first-pass SOW draft to refine
- A change order generator: describe what changed in scope, get a formatted change order ready to send
- An HR document generator: paste a role description or requirements, get a job description or onboarding doc draft
- A document review / key terms extractor: paste a contract or vendor document, get a plain-language summary of key obligations, dates, and flags
- A proposal or pitch draft: describe the client, the opportunity, and RDC's relevant experience, get a first-pass narrative

Confidentiality note: if the person expresses concern about putting sensitive business information into an AI tool, acknowledge that honestly. Claude.ai does not store or train on conversation data, but the decision about what to share is always theirs.
