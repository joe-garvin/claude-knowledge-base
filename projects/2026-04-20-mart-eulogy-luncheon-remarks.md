## Project brief

### Context

Mart died March 22, 2025, age 39. These are not a formal eulogy delivered at the funeral Mass, but post-Mass luncheon remarks — one of three sets of remarks delivered by Joe, Matt, and Steve. Brothers will need to coordinate to avoid overlap or contradiction. The luncheon setting calls for something conversational rather than ceremonial.

### Approach

Rather than speaking from personal memory and subjective impression, Joe wants to synthesize and reflect back the picture of Mart that emerged from messages, emails, and notes collected from friends, cousins, and family members across all chapters of Mart's life. The remarks speak for the broader community of people who loved him, not just from one brother's vantage point.

### Core themes (drawn from source material)

Three themes kept surfacing in what people sent:

1. **Joy and fun.** Mart was fun-loving, merry, easy to be around, made rooms more interesting just by being in them. *Caveat: when manic, Mart was the opposite of easy to be around — this needs careful handling. Use judicious adjectives rather than broad claims about his personality.*

2. **Genuine care for people.** He was thoughtful and looked after people. Examples from source material: stayed behind at a party to look after a friend (Brad) who was sick from drinking instead of leaving for another party. Checked in on Mike Meddick when Mike's parents were divorcing — "How's Mike doing? I hope he's okay." The phrase that recurred: *"Marty was always good to me."*

3. **Coolness and personal style.** He had an intentional, cohesive, urbane sense of style. People described him as effortlessly cool, charismatic, put-together.

### Open question: the illness and struggle

Whether and how to acknowledge Mart's illness and struggle. Some people mentioned it in their notes. Joe used it as context when reaching out to people to explain the circumstances of Mart's death. Possible angle: he's at peace now, no longer struggling. Joe is uncertain whether this belongs in the remarks at all. Claude will offer a recommendation on this question after reviewing the source material.

### Format and delivery constraints

- Target length: 3–4 minutes; approximately 4–5 paragraphs. Joe is open to Claude's recommendation on whether this is the right call given the occasion and content.
- Will be delivered aloud — must be easy to speak, not read
- Neutral, understated delivery; no emotional high-wire moments built into the text
- Joe may be quite emotional on the day; remarks should not require him to hold it together through difficult passages
- Plain spoken. Simple language. Conversational and earnest. No compound-complex sentences. No advertising cadence, no slickness, no quotable soundbites.
- Avoid AI writing patterns throughout (skill: avoid-ai-writing)

### Source material

A master document compiling notes, messages, and emails from friends and family will be the primary input. Joe will share this before the outline stage.

### Claude's role

Beyond executing Joe's direction, Claude will actively offer analysis and recommendations on: length and format, whether and how to address the illness/struggle question, what additional themes from the source material are worth drawing on alongside the three Joe has already identified, and anything else in the material that seems worth including.
