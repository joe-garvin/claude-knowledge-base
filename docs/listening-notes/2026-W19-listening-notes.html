Polygonia
Ceaseless Motion
TimedanceTechno · IDMMay 2026

Lindsey Wang's new EP on Batu's Timedance label, described as four "restive technoid anomalies" dancing in the gaps between Objekt, Gyrofield, and 33EMYBW. Boomkat's language is precise: "tricksy club torque," a finely calibrated rhythmic engineering. Polygonia has been in RA's orbit for a while but this is her first Timedance credit, and Timedance is exactly the kind of label that belongs here — releases like Blawan, Objekt, Parris all share something with the tightly wound, intellectual side of the collection. No prior library presence; unknown artist.

Duori
StroomElectroacoustic · World · ExperimentalMay 2026

PAN alum Daniele Guerrini (Heith) and Bandung's Teguh Permana (Tarawangsawelas), making what they call "5th world music in low data mode" — recorded during ceremonies in Bandung and later refined on tour. Stroom is a Tier 2 label, and a record that's been brewing since 2017 between a PAN artist and a Morphine Records alumnus carries exactly the kind of conceptual and geographical weight this collection is drawn to. Both sources covered it. The slow development and geographic specificity (Bandung, West Java) feel like strong signals.

New
Bergsonist
Depths
Dark EntriesTechno · Beatdown · MaghrebMay 2026

Moroccan-born Selwa Abd's debut on Dark Entries — 12 tracks of what Boomkat calls syncopated Maghreb-techno, rugged beatdown, and dreamy post-rave. The conceptual framing (postcolonial identity, speculative temporalities) and the references (DJ Haram, DJ Plead, Teresa Winter) point toward something with genuine friction and specificity. Bergsonist has been prolific on Bandcamp for years but this Dark Entries LP marks a more considered moment. Dark Entries is adjacent to the collection's industrial/synth-wave edges; the techno here sits closer to the rhythmic minimalism end than the functional peak-time end.

02New Releases from Artists You Know ×2

Vladislav Delay Quintet
Vd5
We JazzJazz · Fusion · ElectronicMay 2026

Sasu Ripatti (Vladislav Delay, also Luomo) is in the Spotify playlists with 3–5 tracks. This is the quintet's second album, 15 years after the quartet record, and Boomkat leads the weekly digest with it — calling out Moritz Von Oswald Trio as a reference alongside Bill Laswell Material productions. Ripatti on drums, with Maria Bertel, Max Loderbauer, Lucio Capece, and Derek Shirley. Boomkat describes it as moving from "slick to tempestuous," defying jazz preconceptions. The We Jazz label and the personnel involved (Loderbauer produced with von Oswald for years) are both strong signals. This is the biggest release in the digest.

03Wildcard ×1
Wildcard
Mika Vainio
Dub Mix 2
Sähkö RecordingsDub · SelectionsMay 2026

A posthumous dub selections tape from one of the defining figures in Finnish electronic music. Mika Vainio — known for his work as Ø and with Ilpo Väisänen in Pan Sonic — recorded this in 1998, cueing up 17 dub cuts focused on stripped versions and bass foundations from his 7" collection. Boomkat describes it as evidence of the "fundamental attention to space, bass, and syncopation" that defined his own output. The wildcard logic here is less about unfamiliar aesthetics and more about form: this is a selections/mixtape document rather than original work, and a posthumous one at that. But given the Pan Sonic / Ø orbit and the dub focus, it's too relevant to file only under Also Noted. Boomkat covered it prominently. Art unavailable via Bandcamp scraping.

04Also Noted ×4
01Shūdan Sokai — その前夜 Live At 八王子 Alone (Aguirre) — First reissue of a 1970s Japanese free jazz document that Boomkat calls "mythical" — a quartet drawing a straight line from NYC loft jazz to Tokyo. Aguirre has strong curatorial credentials for this kind of reissue. Filed here because it's a historical document rather than new work, but it's a significant one for anyone tracking the Japanese avant-garde lineage.
02F~M — Fose (Old Technology / Surgeon) — Roel Funcken (Funckarma, Skam) and Jeroen Bax (Exm) on Surgeon's Old Technology imprint: an hour of freeform modular electronics with Skam-era echoes. Cross-sourced in both Boomkat emails. The Skam connection and the Surgeon label are both adjacent; scored just below the cutoff for a main pick on label tier.
03Schweres Wasser — Bleeding Immaculate EP (Whispering Signals) — Orthodox dub techno from Aachen (NRW) by Thorsten Hoffmann & Markus Offermann, with a Vril remix. Described as Deepchord-level padded dub chord insulation. Sat in the archive for 10 years before release. No label tier match but the aesthetic fit is strong.
04Satoshi & Makoto — Mirage Cafe (8mm Records) — A decade after their Safe Trip CZ-5000 sides, the (possibly apocryphal) Japanese twins return on Young Marco's 8mm. Boomkat draws a direct line to The Other People Place's Lifestyles of the Laptop Cafe — blissed electro-house downbeat with YMO elegance. The Other People Place is in the local library. Worth a listen for that reason alone.
