01New Discoveries ×4
New
Shinetiac
Not All Who Wander Are Lost
West Mineral Ltd.Downtempo · IDM · AmbientMay 2026

Finally on vinyl: Pontiac Streator, Shiner (Jari Cain Rosenthal), and Ben Bondy's West Mineral debut, newly mastered by Rashad Becker in an edition of 300. The trio's overlapping circles — Streator's lo-fi digital work, Bondy's dissolving textures, Rosenthal's sampling instincts — converge on something warmer and more song-adjacent than any of them individually. Boomkat draws the line from Hysterical Love Project to Seefeel to Arovane: cybernetic Trip Hop with a gooey core. The original was digital-only in 2023; this vinyl pressing gives it the physical object it was waiting for.

New
Memotone
Warm Shadows
Accidental MeetingsAmbient · Jazz · Fourth WorldMay 2026

Bristol's Will Yates has been in an expansive run — somewhere between ambient house, jazz production, and the fourth-world territory Jon Hassell mapped out. Warm Shadows goes further in that direction, with a cast of Bristol collaborators (Ugnė Uma, Typesun & Jabu) giving the record a specific social and geographic texture. Boomkat draws the line to Moritz Von Oswald Trio, The Necks, Laurel Halo — which is accurate but undersells how patient and particular this record is. Cross-sourced in both the weekly digest and Download Now.

New
Natalia Panzer
The Loss Of Small Detail
BlushElectroacoustic · Spoken Word · AmbientMay 2026

Tape and book object, edition of 100, from Glasgow's blush label — affiliated with Somewhere Press, Lunchtime Gallery, the same thoughtful curatorial orbit. Panzer is better known as one half of ana reme alongside Voice Actor's Levi Lanser; this is a solo piece, dreamy spoken word over loops and samples, presented twice: once with the full musical backdrop, once as voice only. Boomkat's reference points are Lucy Van, Félicia Atkinson, Blue Jam. The "pseudoaudiobook" framing shouldn't put you off — the music is the context, not decoration.

New
Akio Suzuki
Soundsphere
Room 40Electroacoustic · ExperimentalMay 2026

Japanese sound artist and inventor of the analapos — a resonating tube instrument — who has been working in this field since the late 1960s, with collaborators ranging from John Cage to Otomo Yoshihide. Soundsphere is presented by Lawrence English on Room 40, and English's framing is accurate: an artist driven by "curiosity as guiding principle," recording in spaces ranging from nuclear reactors to concert halls, seeking the unexpected. This is not ambient in any easy sense — there's a rigorous attention to site and material that gives every sound a reason to be there.

02New Releases from Artists You Know ×2
In Library
Marc Leclair
Musique Pour 3 Femmes Enceintes
Mutek / ISC Hi Fi SelectsMicro-House · AmbientMay 2026 (orig. 2005)

Leclair is in the local library as Akufen — Quebec Nightclub on Perlon, Fabric 17, the micro-sampling methodology that put Montreal on the micro-house map. This 2005 LP under his own name moved in the opposite direction: slower, more atmospheric, more personal. The occasion was several women in his circle becoming pregnant simultaneously; the record is what he made in response. Boomkat's description is apt: "precisely puckered micro-house" still operating, but at a lower resolution, with more room to breathe. First vinyl edition, first streaming availability — this one waited two decades for its proper physical release.

In Playlists
Crys Cole & Oren Ambarchi / Giuseppe Ielasi
Sparkling Or Silent / Unfamiliar Music (Paris)
Portraits GRMElectroacoustic · LowercaseMay 2026

Portraits GRM is the label Peter Rehberg established in collaboration with Shelter Press and INA GRM — focused entirely on pieces commissioned by the institution. Ambarchi is already in the playlists (two tracks); this is where that thread leads. Side A: Cole and Ambarchi in "quiet, shivering miniature," an electroacoustic method that draws within the sound itself — "reminiscences, impressions and sensations weaving a narrative." Side B: Giuseppe Ielasi transforming a live Paris set into lowercase psychedelic harmonics. Two different angles on the same institutional commission format, neither of them background music.

03Wildcard ×1
Wildcard
Jeff Parker And ETA IVtet
Happy Today
International Anthem / NonesuchJazz · ImprovisationMay 2026

International Anthem out of Chicago — same label that's been releasing Makaya McCraven, Ben LaMar Gay, Irreversible Entanglements — and Jeff Parker leading the ETA IVtet (Jay Bellerose, Anna Butterss, Josh Johnson) in two sprawling LP side-length pieces recorded live at Lodge Room, Highland Park, Los Angeles. The wildcard logic is straightforward: this is outside the collection's usual instrumental language, acoustic-forward and jazz-rooted, but International Anthem's patience and structural ambition are consistent signals. "Minimalist, form-bending improvisational syntax" is their phrase; the first ETA IVtet recording outside the now-closed micro-venue they were named for.

04Also Noted ×5
01Gramm (Jan Jelinek) — Personal Rock (Faitiche) — Jelinek's finest Gramm LP, "more resolved and driven than Loop-Finding Jazz Records" per Boomkat. First proper reissue; the Faitiche catalog sits adjacent to the collection's loopy, textural side. Reissue bar applies but this one has a clear hook.
02Martyna Basta — Winged In Collapse (7K!) — Third album, bigger cast than previous work, featuring James K, Rainy Miller, LEYA, Felisha Ledesma. Boomkat featured it prominently: "quietly grand," the intersection of sacred music, baroque, and contemporary dreampop. Sits slightly outside the electronic core but the label has credibility.
03Function × Nastia Reigel — Devocion (Dekmantel) — Dave Sumner in his Sandwell District lineage, now in acid-breakbeat mode alongside Nastia Reigel. Dekmantel is Tier 2; the aesthetic lands closer to the industrial edge of the collection than the deep/dub end. Cross-sourced in both Boomkat emails.
04Mick Harris — Culvert Dub Session Seven (L.I.E.S.) — Seventh in the Scorn / Napalm Death alumnus's ongoing L.I.E.S. series — slow-grinding industrial dub from Birmingham's most stubborn machine-music practitioner. Tier 3 label; the aesthetic sits at the heavier end of dub but not entirely outside the picture.
05Parris — Drippin' EP (Can You Feel The Sun) — Two tracks in the playlists; bar is higher. His and Call Super's label, woodblock-driven grooves that go from canter to drift. Not enough for a main section pick but the personnel are consistent signals — worth a listen given the Hessle Audio adjacency.

The Marc Leclair reissue is the week's quiet revelation — a record that waited twenty years for its vinyl moment. Shinetiac and Memotone lead the new discoveries, and the Portraits GRM split is the kind of release that only exists because institutional electroacoustic commissions occasionally produce exactly what you needed.
