01New Discoveries ×5
New
Boards of Canada
Tape 05
WarpIDM · Ambient · ElectronicMay 2026

Thirteen years after Tomorrow's Harvest, BOC returns on Warp with what RA calls "some of their most beautiful work yet." That's not a phrase RA applies carelessly, especially to a duo whose absence has lasted long enough that any return carries the risk of anti-climax. Not formally in the known collection, though the artists closest to the collection's center — Aphex Twin, Jon Hassell, Cluster — all share territory with the BOC aesthetic: saturated textures, false memory, music that sounds like it was recorded somewhere else and some other time. The title suggests a cassette document, archival in feel. Warp (Tier 1) plus RA Recommends puts this at the top of the scoring table for this week.

New
K Wata
Give U Space
Short SpanDub · Techno · HouseMay 2026

Boomkat's framing for this NYC debut LP is exactly right: "nocturnal dub topographies." That's a specific register — not dub as style, but dub as spatial practice, as negative space and bass weight — and it's one that runs through a significant part of this collection (Shinichi Atobe, Kyle Hall, DJ Sprinkles). Short Span isn't a Tier 1/2 label by current classification, but the editorial confidence from Boomkat's feature slot carries real weight. "Give U Space" as a title does the same work the music apparently does: rooms to move in, air around the kick.

New
Planetary Assault Systems
Planetary People
Ostgut TonTechno · BleepMay 2026

Luke Slater's PAS alias returns to Ostgut Ton, and Boomkat describes it as "returning to a club landscape that he helped spawn" — which is accurate. PAS's bleep-techno lineage connects the early 90s UK scene to the Berlin axis without quite belonging to either, and that tension has always been part of what makes the alias interesting. Not in the known collection formally, but this is one of those cases where the name recognition is inverse to the library presence: Slater's work has been canonical for long enough that a new PAS record is a genuine event. Ostgut Ton (Tier 2) with this pedigree behind it.

New
Function × Nastia
Devoción
DekmantelTechno · Acid · BreakbeatMay 2026

Dave Sumner (Function, Sandwell District) and Ukrainian DJ/producer Nastia collaborate for Dekmantel (Tier 2). Boomkat's read: "gliding acid-breakbeat-techno." The Sandwell District lineage is one of austere, almost devotional techno — conceptual, slow-burning, more interested in atmosphere than impact — and the title Devoción leans into that register deliberately. Nastia's club-facing sensibility adds some dynamic range. The acid element is a positive signal in this collection; the breakbeat structure keeps it from reading as functional peak-time material. A strong label pairing whatever the outcome.

New
Shinetiac
Not All Who Wander Are Lost
West MineralDowntempo · Trip Hop · AmbientMay 2026

Shiner, Pontiac Streator, and Ben Bondy debut as Shinetiac on West Mineral — Huerco S.'s label, whose early work sits in the local library. All three are deep in the neo-ambient orbit (3XL, Quiet Time, Motion Ward) and the Boomkat framing is precise: "downtempo pleasure, re-imagining classic Trip Hop with a gooey cybernetic core." The reference line — Hysterical Love Project to Seefeel, Olive to A.S.O., Ulla to Arovane — is an unusual one, holding both the hazy 90s trip-hop strand and the more introspective neo-ambient one in the same frame. Newly mastered by Rashad Becker. Edition of 300 on vinyl. West Mineral's label history alone is reason to pay attention.

02New Releases from Artists You Know ×1
In Playlists
Parris
Drippin' EP
Can You Feel The SunTechno · HouseMay 2026

Parris has 2 tracks in the Spotify playlists — borderline known, treated here as a flag for new work rather than a discovery. Can You Feel The Sun is his label co-run with Call Super, which gives it adjacent-label status even if it's not in the formal tier list. Boomkat's description: "scaling from brisk canter to woodblock-driven waft." That phrase does real work — "woodblock-driven" signals something rhythmically specific and non-functional, and the transition from canter to waft suggests a record that breathes rather than hammers. Short EP, worth a focused listen.

03Wildcard ×1
Wildcard
Jeff Parker & ETA IVtet
Happy Today
International AnthemJazz · Post-Rock · FusionMay 2026

Jeff Parker (Tortoise, solo on Nonesuch) brings the ETA IVtet to International Anthem — one of the most consistently interesting experimental jazz labels running. Boomkat describes it as a "post-rock legend" assembling a quartet with Jay Bellerose, Ann Wood, and others for a live recording. International Anthem has put out significant records by Irreversible Entanglements, Makaya McCraven, Rob Mazurek; Parker sitting in that company feels right. The wildcard logic is about format as much as aesthetic: this is an extended live document, jazz-adjacent rather than electronic, but the conceptual continuity between Parker's solo work (layered guitar, sampler-as-instrument) and this collection's own jazz fringe (Jon Hassell, Alice Coltrane) makes it a natural stretch rather than a reach.

04Also Noted ×6
01Memotone — Warm Shadows (Accidental Meetings) — The weekly digest confirms this one more strongly than the Download Now email did. Will Yates has been building the Memotone sound across TTT, Sähkö, and World of Echo LPs, and Boomkat calls this "4th world ambient jazz" with Ugné Uma, Typesun & Jabu as presences — FFO Moritz Von Oswald Trio, The Necks, Laurel Halo. Nearly a main pick; the label tier kept it here.
02Mick Harris — Culvert Dub Session Seven (L.I.E.S.) — Tier 3 label, 30 years of Scorn/Napalm Death drummer doing his own thing under his name. Boomkat's read is "Rhythm & Sound-esque" with crust and grime on top — RIYL The Bug, Ghost Dubs, Pessimist. Industrial dub with a strong lineage, worth a listen if you're in a heavy mood.
03Gramm (Jan Jelinek) — Personal Rock (Faitiche) — First vinyl reissue of Jelinek's 1999 solo LP outside the Akufen alias; Boomkat describes it as "more resolved and driven than Loop-Finding Jazz Records." Akufen is in the local library so this is effectively a known-artist reissue. The comparison to SND's Tender Love and Alva Noto places it precisely.
04Sergeant — Symbols (Stroom) — Belgian avant-pop outfit's second album on Tier 2 label Stroom, references Milan W and Tota. Strong label match; scored just below the New Discovery cutoff.
05Martyn — Music for Existing (3024) — Weekly confirms the weekly digest is enthusiastic: "refined yet expansive," collaborators include Duval Timothy, Lucinda Chua. Jazz-rooted electronic, worth the listen if that venn diagram resonates.
06Speedy J — Walkman (Stoor) — "Wildest album since 2000's A Shocking Hobby." A veteran doing something unhinged; worth a note even without a label tier match.
