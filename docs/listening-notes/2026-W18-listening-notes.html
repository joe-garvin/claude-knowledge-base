01New Discoveries ×4
New
Seefeel
Sol.Hz
Warp RecordsDub · Ambient · Post-rockMay 2026

Described by Boomkat as Seefeel’s “dub album,” Sol.Hz is bass-heavy and spare, building on the percussive mood they opened up on Everything Squared. Warp, dub, longform atmospherics — this should be squarely in range. The comparison to their track “(Ch-Vox)” is a good pointer: this is for headphone listening, not the floor.

New
Intertoto
Siccar Point
What About NeverDub Techno · AmbientMay 2026

Boomkat flags this as a must for dub techno listeners — Intertoto’s debut for Secondo and Capracara’s label, described as organic and geologic, referencing early Vladislav Delay and Short Span. The conceit (allegorical study of the Scottish North Sea coast, impressionistic scapes) matches the kind of conceptual weight the collection favors. Debut artist; no prior catalog hits.

New
Sentomea
Wonderment EP
MOS RecordingsDeep House · Detroit TechnoMay 2026

Sander Schuurman’s follow-up to a 2017 debut on Delsin, described as channeling classic Dutch techno and deep house with “wow & flutter wooziness” and slow-burn romanticism. MOS is Aroy Dee’s imprint — Delsin-adjacent territory. Boomkat name-checks NWAQ and Terrace. This sounds like exactly the kind of hardware-based, emotionally specific deep house the collection wants more of.

02New Releases from Artists You Know ×2
In Library
Shinichi Atobe
Silent Way
Plastic & SoundsDeep House · Dub TechnoMay 2026

Atobe is in the local library — Love of Plastic and Discipline both owned. Silent Way is his 8th album, self-released on Plastic & Sounds after a long run on Chain Reaction and DDS. Boomkat leads the digest with it: lush, hypnotic deep house and beatdown dub techno in long-form. By any measure a significant new statement from one of the few artists in the collection who operates at this level of sustained introspection.

In Playlists
Yu Su
Foundry
Short SpanAmbient · Kosmische · DubMay 2026

Yu Su is in the Spotify playlists (extended, 3–5 tracks). Foundry is her second LP — spacious kosmische synths with dubby basslines, her own breathy vocals, and one concrete nod toward the dancefloor on the title track. Covered by both Boomkat and Bandcamp’s May 1 Essential Releases. The Short Span label is in the orbit of CS + Kreme and PPU — consistent with the collection’s quieter edges.

03Wildcard ×1
Wildcard
Rabit
Stranger in a Strange Land
Halcyon VeilTrip-hop · Experimental · DJ Screw-influencedMay 2026

Sits well outside the usual footprint — trip-hop isn’t a genre in the collection, and Halcyon Veil isn’t a label on the radar. But Boomkat leads the whole digest with it and the references are interesting: DJ Screw worship applied to Lynch and Badalamenti samples, with Andy Stott, Basinski, and Boards of Canada’s Old Tapes era all invoked. Guest spot from Lolina. The emotional texture (codeine-soaked, liminal, slow) is aesthetically adjacent even if the genre isn’t. Worth at least one listen to see if it lands.

04Also Noted ×4
01Masayoshi Fujita & Jan Jelinek — Faitiche Edition No.3 (Faitiche) — Live recording from Kyoto 2025, abstract jazz-exotica vibraphone and electronics. Tape-only. Didn’t quite score high enough on the profile match to make the main picks.

04Theo Parrish — Lovely Edits Vol.1 (Lovely Edits) — Official licensed version of the Ugly Edits bootleg series. Not new work, but a significant release for the Chicago/Detroit deep house lineage that’s central to the collection.
