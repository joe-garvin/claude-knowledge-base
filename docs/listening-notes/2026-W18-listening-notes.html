Seefeel
Sol.Hz
WarpDub · Ambient · Post-RockMay 2026

Described by Boomkat as Seefeel's "dub album," Sol.Hz is bass-heavy and spare, building on the percussive mood they opened up on Everything Squared. Warp, dub, longform atmospherics — this should be squarely in range. The comparison to their track "(Ch-Vox)" is a good pointer: this is for headphone listening, not the floor.

MOS Recordings (Aroy Dee's label)Deep House · Detroit TechnoMay 2026

Sander Schuurman's follow-up to a 2017 debut on Delsin, described as channeling classic Dutch techno and deep house with "wow & flutter wooziness" and slow-burn romanticism. MOS is Aroy Dee's imprint — Delsin-adjacent territory. Boomkat name-checks NWAQ and Terrace. This sounds like exactly the kind of hardware-based, emotionally specific deep house the collection wants more of.

02New Releases from Artists You Know ×2
Shinichi Atobe
In Library
Shinichi Atobe
Silent Way
Plastic & Sounds (Atobe's own label)Deep House · Dub TechnoMay 2026

Atobe is in the local library — Love of Plastic and Discipline both owned. Silent Way is his 8th album, self-released on Plastic & Sounds after a long run on Chain Reaction and DDS. Boomkat leads the digest with it: lush, hypnotic deep house and beatdown dub techno in long-form. A significant new statement from one of the few artists in the collection who operates at this level of sustained introspection.

In Playlists
Yu Su
Foundry
Short SpanAmbient · Kosmische · DubMay 2026

Yu Su is in the Spotify playlists (extended, 3–5 tracks). Foundry is her second LP — spacious kosmische synths with dubby basslines, her own breathy vocals, and one concrete nod toward the dancefloor on the title track. Covered by both Boomkat and Bandcamp's May 1 Essential Releases, which adds confidence. The Short Span label is consistent with the collection's quieter edges.

03Wildcard ×1
Rabit
Wildcard
Rabit
Stranger in a Strange Land
Halcyon VeilTrip-Hop · Experimental · DJ Screw-influencedMay 2026

Sits well outside the usual footprint — trip-hop isn't a genre in the collection, and Halcyon Veil isn't a label on the radar. But Boomkat leads the whole digest with it and the references are interesting: DJ Screw worship applied to Lynch and Badalamenti samples, with Andy Stott, Basinski, and Boards of Canada's Old Tapes era all invoked. Guest spot from Lolina. The emotional texture (codeine-soaked, liminal, slow) is aesthetically adjacent even if the genre isn't.
