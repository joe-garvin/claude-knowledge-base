01New Discoveries ×4
R. Boy
New
R. Boy
Trax From the Bottom of a Well
Self-released (tape)Deep House · Garage-Blues

Manchester DJ/producer Finn's debut LP under this new alias — nine house elegies sitting somewhere between garage-blues, lo-fi soul, and electronic introspection. Boomkat clusters the references around Actress, Kassem Mosse, Omar-S, and Rezzett. The 4-track recording style gives it grain and personality. Finn has history as a DJ but this is a fresh name; treat as new.

G Version III
New
G Version III
Chapter II
Good Morning TapesDigidub · Steppers · FM Synthesis

Kyoto-based G Version III continues what began with a Riddim Chango 12" last year — minor-key drill synths fused with digi-steppers and holographic FM textures. Good Morning Tapes is a well-curated label; the reference cluster hits Equiknoxx, John T. Gast, TNT Roots. Not in collection. This is exactly the kind of record that rewards patient listening — not peak-time, not anthemic.

John Howes builds with custom software — fractal chain reactions with an unusually organic quality. Boomkat name-drops Carrier, Sasu Ripatti, and Purelink, which puts this in the same zone as Skee Mask's more abstract tendencies and the left edge of Perlon. The Peak Oil connection adds credibility.

03Wildcard ×1
Christina Kubisch
Wildcard
Christina Kubisch
Tuning
Faitiche (Jan Jelinek)Electroacoustic · Electromagnetic

Kubisch has been working with electromagnetic interference and tuning forks since the '70s — this Faitiche anthology collects a recording from her "Electrical Walks" series alongside tuning fork compositions and recent material. Faitiche's curation standards are high (Roméo Poirier, Farida, Panoram). This sits further out than the typical digest pick — closer to the electroacoustic and sound art edge. Worth the risk.
