Music From MemoryShoegaze · Dub · Electro · Trip-Hop2026 reissue of 1991 recordings

1991 recordings by A.R. Kane's Rudy Tambala and Cranes vocalist Alison Shaw, finally reissued by Music From Memory with previously unreleased material. Boomkat calls it "the most essential Music From Memory release in a hot minute" — shoegaze, dub, electro, and trip-hop in that early-'90s pre-Seefeel zone. The A.R. Kane lineage alone makes this essential listening.

New
Juan Atkins & Moritz Von Oswald / Borderland
Transport
TresorDub Techno · Detroit/BerlinApril 2026

Three years on from the first Borderland session, the two architects of Detroit and Berlin techno reconvene on Tresor for seven expansive techno dub pieces. Boomkat highlights the less conventional pieces that push the edges of both producers' ranges — which is exactly where this intersection gets interesting. Tresor, dub, Detroit lineage, longform: this checks every box.

Japanese enigma Meitei reworks his cult Kofū trilogy with augmented results shaped by touring environments. The reference points — plunderphonic reworkings of Japanese folk and theatre music, described as adjacent to The Caretaker and Moon Wiring Club — suggest conceptual weight and textural depth. Unknown artist to the collection; the aesthetic match is strong.

Buttechno (Pavel Milyakov) is a high-frequency Spotify artist. X Berg Dubs 2 is new work on his own Psy X label — kosmische arps, grimy steppas, and a sideways dark room hard house cut. Short format (4 tracks), loose and in-the-mix, but Boomkat says these work as a standalone session too. His cult City-2 sound is still very much intact.

In Playlists
Irmin Schmidt
Requiem
MutePrepared Piano · Electroacoustic · ExperimentalApril 2026

CAN is in the Spotify playlists (high frequency). Requiem is Irmin Schmidt's new album of spontaneous duets between prepared piano and nature — a nightingale recorded outside his studio, ambient environmental sound. The Cage/Feldman/Annea Lockwood references position it firmly in the introspective, conceptual end of the collection's range. Boomkat seems genuinely moved by it.

03Wildcard ×1
Carla Dal Forno
Wildcard
Carla Dal Forno
Confession
KallistaDub-inflected DIY Pop · Post-PunkApril 2026

Not in the collection. Dal Forno's fourth album was recorded in an abandoned hospital — rattly, dub-inflected vignettes about a friendship turned romance. Young Marble Giants and Broadcast in the reference orbit, with a raw directness that sits adjacent to the collection's quieter corners. The DIY dub angle is the hook; worth a listen on the strength of the Broadcast comparison alone.

04Also Noted ×4
01Rhythm & Sound — The Versions (Burial Mix / Basic Channel) — all-time essential dub techno, back in print. Worth flagging the repress if not in the library.
02The Caretaker — Patience (After Sebald) (History Always Favours The Winners) — back in print after 5 years. Significant in the ambient/hauntology orbit. Repress only.
