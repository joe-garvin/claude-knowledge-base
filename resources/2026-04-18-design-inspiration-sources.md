# Design inspiration sources

**Date:** 2026-04-18
**Source:** Claude conversation
**Folder:** resources

**Summary:** Curated list of design inspiration sources organized by use case — personal portfolio website (writer-focused), general web design, designer portfolios and process, document/presentation templates, and flyer design. Compiled with high-standard, industry-aware sources in mind rather than generic roundups.

---

## Portfolio websites — for writers specifically

**[format.com/magazine](https://www.format.com/magazine/galleries/design/writing-portfolio-roundup)**
Roundups of standout writer portfolio sites with design breakdowns. Good starting point for seeing the range of what works for writers.

**[webflow.com/blog/writer-websites](https://webflow.com/blog/writer-websites)**
Five well-chosen examples with analysis of what makes each one work — layout, typography, animation, navigation. Webflow-built, so design quality is high.

**[siteinspire.com](https://www.siteinspire.com)**
Hand-curated since 2010. One of the most consistently high-quality galleries on the web. Favors clean, editorial, typographically sophisticated designs — generous whitespace, restrained color, strong typography. Filter by "portfolio" type. Best place to look for aesthetic direction.

## General web design inspiration — where designers actually look

**[godly.website](https://godly.website)**
Curates the most visually impressive sites on the internet, with extremely high standards — 3–5 new sites per week rather than daily. Animated thumbnails let you preview motion before clicking through. Go-to for creative agencies on high-end projects.

**[awwwards.com](https://www.awwwards.com)**
Most recognized web design award platform. Every nominated site is scored on design, usability, creativity, and content by a panel of industry judges. Real value is in reading the judge scores — you see what working professionals value and why. Skews toward animation-heavy, high-budget work.

**[httpster.net](https://httpster.net)**
Curates clean, modern sites with a bias toward independent designers, small studios, and creative professionals. Leans toward understated elegance — portfolio sites, type foundries, design studios. Feels personal and opinionated. Good counterweight to Awwwards.

**[are.na](https://www.are.na)**
Not a gallery — a collective research tool where designers publicly save and organize visual references. Following the right channels gives you a window into how people with strong taste actually think. High signal-to-noise once you find the right contributors.

## Designer work and process

**[behance.net](https://www.behance.net)**
In-depth project case studies showing full design process from brief to final product. Useful for understanding design decisions, not just end results. Search "poster design," "document template," "flyer" for relevant work.

**[dribbble.com](https://dribbble.com)**
Polished visual shots optimized for quick visual impact — like Instagram for designers. Better for spotting color and aesthetic trends than studying process. Good for flyer and layout mood-boarding.

---

## Document and presentation templates

**[notion.so/templates](https://www.notion.so/templates)**
Designers have published polished layouts for client-facing docs — outlines, briefs, structured copy documents. Good reference for what a clean, presentable copy doc can look like.

**[lapa.ninja](https://www.lapa.ninja)**
Over 7,000 full-page screenshots with filters by industry, layout, and year. Landing page focus, but the layout thinking transfers well to document structure and hierarchy.

**[land-book.com](https://land-book.com)**
Similar to Lapa — practical and polished landing page examples. Good for studying layout decisions.

For slide deck aesthetics specifically: search "presentation template" or "slide deck design" on Behance and Dribbble for curated examples.

---

## For the Recovery Dharma flyer

Behance is the best bet — search "event poster," "community flyer," or "spiritual design."

**[typewolf.com](https://www.typewolf.com)**
Typography inspiration and font pairing recommendations. Typography does a lot of the visual work in a good flyer. Worth bookmarking separately.

---

## Passive intake

**[Muzli](https://muz.li)** (browser extension)
Delivers design inspiration directly to your new tab feed — collects trends, news, and case studies from around the web. Personalizable by style or discipline. Good for passive intake without actively hunting.
