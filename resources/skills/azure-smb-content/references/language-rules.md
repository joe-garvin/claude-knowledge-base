# Language rules

## Words and phrases to use

| Use this | Instead of |
|----------|------------|
| Business / company | Organization |
| Technology / technology setup / existing systems | IT estate / IT infrastructure |
| Growing businesses / businesses of all sizes | SMB (in titles and headlines) |
| Medium | Midsize |
| Modernize your systems / upgrade and simplify | Digital transformation |
| At your own pace / ready when you are | Urgency-first language |
| Businesses of all sizes | Size-exclusive language |
| Practical / right away / immediate | Abstract or long-horizon language |
| Enterprise-grade security (when needed) | "Enterprise" as general audience framing |
| Grow / save time / stay competitive | Innovate / Innovation (for Classic SMB) |

---

## Words and phrases to avoid

- **"Organization"** — too formal, too enterprise; use "business" or "company"
- **"IT estate"** or **"data estate"** — enterprise jargon; use "technology setup," "existing systems," or "current technology"
- **"Enterprise"** — use only when describing an attribute (e.g., "enterprise-grade security"), never as an audience or aspiration frame
- **"Frontier Firm"** — does not resonate with Classic SMB; if used, must include a brief explainer
- **"Innovation" / "Innovate"** — too abstract for Classic SMB; tie to tangible outcomes (time saved, cost reduced, customers served) or replace with "grow," "improve," or "compete"
- **"Digital transformation"** — implies long-term planning; Classic SMBs don't think this way; use "modernize your systems," "upgrade," or "simplify"
- **"SMB" in titles or headlines** — businesses over 500 employees may not identify with the term; use "growing businesses," "businesses of all sizes," or "business growth" in titles; "SMB" can appear within body copy for Classic SMB or Azure Conversation-level content
- **"AI" as a standalone concept** — must be tied to tangible outcomes (automation, time savings, cost reduction, simplicity); do not lead with AI capabilities alone
- **Long-term roadmap language** — "strategic roadmap," "multi-year plan," "journey to X" implies planning horizons Classic SMBs don't have
- **Overly casual or consumer-y tone** — SMBs are businesses; content should feel professional and credible, not startup-casual

---

## Inclusive sizing language

- For content targeting all SMB sizes (25–999 employees): use "growing businesses," "businesses of all sizes," "business growth"
- For Classic SMB specifically (under ~500 employees): "SMB" is acceptable within body copy and introductions
- Upper medium SMBs (500+ employees): frame as "growing businesses," not "SMB"
- Preferred size term: "medium" (not "midsize")

## Enterprise-to-SMB transformation table

| Enterprise | SMB-adapted |
|-----------|-------------|
| "Transform your enterprise IT estate" | "Modernize your business technology" |
| "Become an AI-first Frontier Firm" | "Make your business AI-ready" |
| "Strategic roadmap for digital transformation" | "Practical steps to modernize at your own pace" |
| "Organization-wide deployment" | "Solutions that work for businesses of all sizes" |
| "Innovative enterprise-grade platform" | "Proven technology built for growing businesses" |
| "Accelerate your innovation journey" | "Get more done with the tools your team already uses" |
| "Drive competitive differentiation" | "Stay competitive and grow your business" |
