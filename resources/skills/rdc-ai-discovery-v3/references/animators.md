# Motion graphics artists and animators — interview guide

## Role context

Motion graphics artists and animators at RDC (Nathan Smith, Jared Hastings, Jeffrey Griffith) produce 2D and 2.5D animated content: animated social videos, motion graphics for explainer videos, animatics, storyboards, and visual treatments. Work typically starts from a brief, a script, or a storyboard.

Common work patterns:
- Receiving a script or brief and building an animatic or storyboard before full production
- Working through multiple rounds of feedback on motion, timing, and visual treatment
- Adapting existing motion templates or past work to new projects
- Coordinating with writers, designers, and video editors on combined deliverables

AI's direct role in creating motion graphics is very limited. The strongest opportunities are in the communication, organizational, and brief-interpretation side of the work.

## Likely friction areas to probe

- **Brief interpretation**: Written briefs that aren't specific about motion direction, style, or feeling
- **Storyboard descriptions**: Writing text descriptions of visual sequences for storyboard approval
- **Feedback interpretation**: Vague feedback on motion work ("make it feel faster", "more energy")
- **Version notes**: Tracking what changed between rounds
- **Client/PM communication**: Explaining technical constraints to non-technical stakeholders

## Question sequence

**Q3 — Work mix** (buttons):
> "What takes up most of your time in a typical project?"

Options: The actual animation and motion work / Storyboarding or animatics before production / Feedback rounds and revisions / Client or PM communication / Something else

**Q4 — Where friction lives** (buttons):
> "What part of the process do you find most tedious or frustrating?"

Options: Getting a brief that doesn't give me enough direction / Interpreting vague feedback and figuring out what to actually change / Writing things up — storyboard notes, version summaries, handoff docs / Managing assets and file organization / Something else

**Q5 — Drill-down** (buttons, branch on Q4 answer):

If "vague briefs":
> "When a brief doesn't give you enough visual direction, what do you usually do?"
Options: Ask questions and wait — which delays things / Make my best interpretation and present options / Search for reference examples on my own / Start something and use the first round of feedback to calibrate / Something else

If "vague feedback":
> "When feedback on motion work is vague, what's hardest about it?"
Options: Figuring out what they actually want from "make it feel more energetic" / When the feedback contradicts what the brief originally said / When multiple people give conflicting feedback / Explaining to stakeholders why certain things aren't technically feasible / Something else

If "writing things up":
> "What written tasks come up most in your work?"
Options: Describing visual sequences for storyboard approval / Version notes — what changed in this round / Handoff notes for editors or other team members / Responding to client feedback in writing / Something else

**Q6 — Open-text moment** (see Step 4 in SKILL.md)

**Q7 — AI experience** (buttons):
> "Have you used Claude or any AI tools for work before?"

Options: Yes, fairly regularly / A few times, with mixed results / Once or twice / Not really

**Q8 — Barrier check** (buttons):
> "What would make an AI tool feel not worth using for your work?"

Options: If it can't actually help with the visual/motion side of things / If it requires a lot of writing or setup from me / If the output needs too much editing / I'm open — tell me what's realistic / Something else

## Output guidance

Strong recommendation candidates for this role:
- A brief-to-questions workflow: paste a brief, get a list of what's unclear and questions to ask the PM
- A vague-feedback translator: paste the feedback, describe what you think it means, get help interpreting and planning a response
- A storyboard description writer: describe a sequence in rough notes, get polished storyboard direction text for client approval
- A version summary generator: paste notes on what changed, get a clean round summary

Be honest in the output document about the limits of AI for motion work. The value is in the written and communication-adjacent tasks.
