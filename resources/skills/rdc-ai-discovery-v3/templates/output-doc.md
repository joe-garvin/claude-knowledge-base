# Output document template

Spec for the branded `.docx` generated at the end of the discovery interview. Read this file in full before generating the document.

---

## Design spec

**Colors (RDC brand):**
| Name | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Squid (deep navy) | `#0D1724` | Header block background, footer text |
| Coral (red-pink) | `#FF4F58` | Accent: header border, section label for top opportunity |
| Mist (light teal) | `#ABDCE0` | Optional accent for secondary section labels |
| Gray (light) | `#F3F3F3` | Background shading for the "what we heard" section |
| White | `#FFFFFF` | Header text, body background |

**Font:** Arial throughout. (Closest available substitute for RDC's brand typeface, Suiss.)

**Page setup:** US Letter (8.5 × 11 in), 1-inch margins on all sides.

---

## Document structure

### Section 1 — Header block
- Full-width paragraph with navy (`#0D1724`) background shading
- "Your AI Opportunity Report" — large, bold Arial, white text
- Person's name — smaller weight, white text, below the title
- Coral (`#FF4F58`) bottom border on this paragraph as a divider

### Section 2 — Here's what we heard
- Light gray (`#F3F3F3`) background shading
- Heading: "Here's what we heard" (or similar — keep it conversational)
- 2–3 sentences reflecting back: their role, where their time goes, and the specific moment they described in the open-text answer
- This section exists to make them feel genuinely heard — not summarized

### Section 3 — Your top opportunity
- Coral (`#FF4F58`) accent on the section label
- Plain-language name as a subheading (not framework language)
- 1–2 sentences: what this opportunity would do for them specifically
- **"Where to start" block:** a concrete, copy-pasteable prompt they can use in Claude right now. Formatted visually distinct (e.g., indented, light background, or bordered paragraph)

### Section 4 — More opportunities worth exploring
- 2–3 additional opportunities, briefer than the top one
- Same shape: plain-language name, one sentence on value, one sentence on first step
- No need for the full "Where to start" treatment — a sentence pointing them toward what to say is enough

### Section 5 — Footer
- Small text: "Generated by Claude · Red Door Collaborative · [date]"

---

## Writing guidelines

**Voice and address:**
- Write directly to the person by name throughout: "Here's what stood out, [name]..." not "The user reported..."
- Warm, direct, collegial. Like a thoughtful colleague who was paying attention, not a report from a system.

**Language constraints:**
- No framework language anywhere: not "friction point," not "use case," not "workflow optimization"
- No corporate hedging: not "may be beneficial," not "could potentially"
- The "Where to start" prompt must be specific enough to use in the next five minutes — not a gesture toward a workflow, but an actual thing to say to Claude

**Scope:**
- This document is for them personally. Not a report to present to leadership.
- Only recommend things they can do in Claude today, without setup or configuration.

**Grounding in the interview:**
- The open-text moment answer (what they described as a recent stuck moment) should be the anchor for the headline recommendation — reference it specifically, not generically.
- Each recommendation should feel like it came from this conversation, not a menu.
