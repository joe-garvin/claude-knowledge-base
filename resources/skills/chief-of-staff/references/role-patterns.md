# Chief of Staff — Role-Specific Patterns

This reference helps the CoS adapt its behavior based on the user's role type.
The role is captured during onboarding and determines which patterns are
most relevant.

---

## Program Manager / Team Lead

**Primary CoS value:** Coordination, status visibility, stakeholder management

**Key patterns:**
- Weekly status deck maintenance across workstreams
- Stakeholder communication drafts and prep
- Cross-workstream dependency tracking
- Meeting recap generation with separated action items
- Milestone and deadline tracking with escalation signals
- Executive summary generation for leadership visibility

**Recurring rhythms to suggest:**
- Monday: Weekly priorities with tiered urgency
- Pre-meeting: Talking points and open items per attendee
- Post-meeting: Structured recaps with owner-tagged actions
- Weekly: Status deck refresh with color-coded health indicators
- Monthly: Retrospective summary of shipped vs. carried items

---

## Creative / Designer

**Primary CoS value:** Brief management, review cycles, asset tracking

**Key patterns:**
- Creative brief intake and organization
- Review cycle tracking (draft → internal review → client review → final)
- Asset inventory by project and deliverable type
- Style guide and brand standard reference
- Feedback consolidation from multiple reviewers
- File management with versioning (v1, v2, final, final-FINAL)

**Recurring rhythms to suggest:**
- Weekly: Active brief status across all projects
- Per-project: Review milestone reminders
- Monthly: Portfolio summary of completed work

---

## Content Strategist / Writer

**Primary CoS value:** Editorial calendar, content pipeline, messaging consistency

**Key patterns:**
- Editorial calendar maintenance
- Content pipeline tracking (ideation → draft → review → publish)
- Messaging framework and key talking points reference
- SEO/keyword tracking and UTM parameter management
- Content repurposing tracking (blog → social → email, etc.)
- Style and voice guide adherence

**Recurring rhythms to suggest:**
- Weekly: Content pipeline status and upcoming deadlines
- Per-piece: Draft review reminders and publish dates
- Monthly: Content performance summary (if metrics available)

---

## Project Manager

**Primary CoS value:** Timeline management, task tracking, client communication

**Key patterns:**
- Project timeline and milestone tracking
- Task assignment and follow-up tracking
- Client communication drafts (status emails, meeting agendas)
- Risk and blocker escalation
- Budget and scope tracking (hours, deliverables)
- Resource allocation across projects

**Recurring rhythms to suggest:**
- Monday: Week-ahead view with task priorities per project
- Pre-client-meeting: Status summary and talking points
- Weekly: Internal status report across all active projects
- Monthly: Project health summary for leadership

---

## Account Manager / Business Development

**Primary CoS value:** Client relationship management, proposal support, pipeline tracking

**Key patterns:**
- Client touchpoint tracking and follow-up reminders
- Proposal and SOW drafting support
- Pipeline status and opportunity tracking
- Meeting prep with client context and history
- Quarterly business review preparation
- Renewal and expansion opportunity flagging

**Recurring rhythms to suggest:**
- Weekly: Client touchpoint review and follow-up list
- Pre-meeting: Client context brief and recent history
- Monthly: Pipeline and revenue status
- Quarterly: QBR preparation materials

---

## Embedded Consultant

**Primary CoS value:** Dual-context navigation, deliverable tracking, knowledge management

**Key patterns:**
- Dual organizational context (RDC + client)
- Client-specific tool and process navigation
- Deliverable tracking against SOW/contract scope
- Knowledge capture from client interactions
- Time tracking alignment (if applicable)
- Internal RDC reporting on engagement status

**Additional considerations:**
- Maintain clear separation between client-confidential and RDC-internal content
- Know which tools belong to which organization
- Track both client deadlines and RDC administrative deadlines (timesheets, etc.)

**Recurring rhythms to suggest:**
- Weekly: Deliverable status against SOW milestones
- Bi-weekly: RDC internal check-in prep
- Monthly: Engagement health summary for RDC leadership

---

## How to Use This Reference

During onboarding, after capturing the user's role:

1. Identify the closest role match from above
2. Use the role's key patterns to shape initial CoS behavior
3. Suggest the role's recurring rhythms during the rhythms onboarding block
4. Adapt over time — users rarely fit one archetype perfectly

The user doesn't need to see this categorization. Just apply the relevant
patterns naturally based on what you learn about their work.
