# Show reference

Static reference for The Azure Essentials Show. Consult this at the start of every task.

---

## Format

- Interview series: 1 host (Thomas Maurer), 1–2 Microsoft SME guests
- Episode length: 5–6 minutes of final content
- Includes visual aids: slides, demos, screen shares, URL overlays
- Posted to YouTube and Microsoft Learn; promoted on LinkedIn

---

## Tone and voice

- Natural conversation between colleagues — not a formal presentation
- Engaging, informative, accessible to technical audiences
- Explain concepts clearly; use analogies when helpful
- Avoid internal acronyms unless unavoidable; if unavoidable, explain them on first use

---

## Technical depth: 100–200 level

- Explain what the product or feature is and why it matters
- Describe how it works at a conceptual level — benefits, not implementation details
- Help viewers understand value and know where to go to learn more
- Use concrete examples; avoid abstract language
- Analogies are encouraged for grounding complex concepts

---

## Audience

- External, public-facing
- Business decision makers, IT decision makers, developers
- Tech-savvy general audience with varied Azure experience
- Global — no regional specificity

---

## Business purpose

- Each episode drives viewers to specific CTAs: documentation, tools, Learn modules
- Content must be evergreen — no time-sensitive references, version numbers, or release dates
