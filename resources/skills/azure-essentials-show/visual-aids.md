# Visual aids

Conventions for all visual aid callouts in outlines and scripts.

---

## Types of callouts

**Slides**
`[visual aid: slide]` or `[visual aid: slide showing X]`
A prepared slide shown during the segment. Be specific when the slide content is known.

**Overlays**
`[OVERLAY url]`
A URL displayed on screen while the speaker mentions it. Use the full URL or short link.

**Lower thirds**
`[LOWER THIRD speaker name/title]` or `[LOWER THIRD url]`
Persistent text displayed at the bottom of the frame. Often paired with overlays for URLs.

**Staging notes**
`[2up]` — two people on screen (standard interview format)
`[3UP]` — three elements on screen, typically host + guest + demo/screenshare

---

## Placement

Visual aid callouts go inline with the dialogue that references them — not at the end of the section. Place the callout at the moment the speaker mentions or transitions to the visual.

At the outline stage, place visual aids at the **beginning** of the section they introduce, not at the end.

---

## When to include

- Always include URL callouts when a guest mentions a specific link or resource
- Always flag demo sections even if specific content is TBD
- Note staging changes (e.g., `[2up]` → `[3UP]`) when a demo opens

---

## Spoken URL format

URLs spoken on camera use this format: "aka dot ms forward slash [shortlink]"

Example: "aka dot ms forward slash Azure Accelerate"

---

## Uncertain demo content

If demo scope is unclear at the outline stage, do not invent content. Use a placeholder with a specific question:

`[demo placeholder — confirm scope: will this show [option A] or [option B]?]`

Better to ask than to script something that gets replaced entirely during SME review.
