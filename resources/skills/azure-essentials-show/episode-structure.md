# Episode structure

Every episode follows this arc. Sections may be combined or reordered slightly based on topic, but the overall shape should be consistent.

---

## 1. Social copy

**Purpose:** Promotional text for LinkedIn and other social channels; also used as the episode teaser.

**Speaker:** No speaker tags — written as Thomas's voice but formatted as plain bullets.

**Content:**
- Establish the business problem or opportunity
- Preview what viewers will learn
- End with a compelling value proposition or call to action

**Format:** 5–6 bullet points, no speaker assignments. See `length-and-pacing.md` for word count target.

---

## 2. Introduction [2up]

**Purpose:** Set up the episode, introduce the guest, state the topic.

**Content:**
- Thomas welcomes viewers by name and references the show
- Thomas introduces the guest by name and role
- Thomas states the episode topic in 1–2 sentences
- Guest acknowledges and expresses enthusiasm (brief)

**Length:** 2–3 exchanges, very brief. Thomas carries most of this section.

---

## 3. Foundation (first body section)

**Purpose:** Answer "what is this?" — establish the fundamental concept before going deeper.

**Content:**
- Thomas asks a direct foundation question: "What exactly is X and what does it do?"
- Guest explains the core concept, often with an analogy or concrete example
- Establish why this matters to the viewer

**Length:** 1–2 exchanges. Guest answer runs 3–5 bullets.

---

## 4. Deeper exploration (body sections 2–3)

**Purpose:** Explore benefits, use cases, how it works, who it's for.

**Content:**
- Thomas asks structured questions that segment the topic
- Guest answers often use internal numbering: "There are three main benefits..."
- Visual aids most commonly appear here
- Each subsection runs 2–4 exchanges

**Note:** This section typically expands to 2–3 distinct topic blocks depending on episode scope.

---

## 5. Demo or walkthrough (if applicable)

**Purpose:** Show the product or process in action.

**Staging:** `[3UP]` when the demo is on screen alongside both speakers; `[visual aid: demo]` for screenshare.

**Content:**
- Guest narrates while walking through the interface or process
- Thomas provides bridging questions to advance the demo
- Keep narration tight — demo sections can run long

**Note:** Demo scope often changes during SME review. If scope is uncertain at outline stage, use a placeholder with specific questions rather than scripting it out. See `visual-aids.md`.

---

## 6. CTA and next steps

**Purpose:** Give viewers specific actions to take after watching.

**Content:**
- Thomas asks: "How should viewers get started?" or "What's the next step?"
- Guest gives 2–3 concrete actions with specific resources
- URLs included with `[OVERLAY]` or `[LOWER THIRD]` notations

---

## 7. Standard outro

**Purpose:** Close the episode consistently.

**Content:**
- Thomas thanks the guest by name
- Thomas directs viewers to resources in the description
- Thomas asks for likes, comments, subscriptions
- Closing line (consistent every episode): "Thanks for watching, and we'll see you next time on the Azure Essentials Show!"
