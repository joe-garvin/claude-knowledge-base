# Source material handling

How to approach the materials provided for each episode.

---

## Common source types

**Microsoft Learn documentation**
Comprehensive and technically accurate. Trust it for factual content and terminology. It tends to be more detailed than needed for 100–200 level content — use it for accuracy, not for phrasing.

**Marketing decks with talk tracks**
Well-developed content with emphasis and framing already applied. These often contain the episode's key messages in near-final form. Treat talk tracks as strong input for talking points.

**Kickoff notes**
Capture strategic priorities, emphasis points, and things to avoid from conversations with PMMs and SMEs. These are directional, not comprehensive — use them to calibrate what to foreground and what to leave out.

---

## Priority and trust

- MS Learn docs and marketing decks are authoritative for technical accuracy
- Kickoff notes reflect what stakeholders want to emphasize — treat them as editorial direction
- When sources conflict, flag the conflict explicitly rather than resolving it silently
- When a source is unclear on a key point, flag it with a specific question

---

## What SME comments are

SME feedback on outlines and scripts often carries the actual episode content — not just editing notes. A comment that rewrites a talking point is source material. Treat it accordingly and incorporate the substance, not just the surface change.

---

## What to do when source materials are thin

- Note specifically what's missing or unclear
- Include placeholder bullets in the outline with explicit questions
- Do not invent technical content to fill gaps
- Ask before starting if a critical piece of information is absent
