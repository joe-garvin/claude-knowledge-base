# Series handling

For episodes that are part of a multi-episode series.

---

## Title pattern

Use "Series Name: Specific Episode Focus" for all episodes in a series.

The series name should be consistent across all episodes. The specific focus after the colon should be unique and follow the standard title rules (action verb, customer-focused, 4–6 words total including the series prefix).

Example series:
- "Azure Cost Estimation: Plan Confidently with the Azure Pricing Calculator"
- "Azure Cost Estimation: Your Strategic Guide to Cloud Pricing"

---

## Callbacks to prior episodes

Later episodes in a series should reference earlier ones when the connection is relevant — but keep callbacks brief and natural.

**Example (from the Strategic Guide script):**
> "As we covered in our first episode in this series, the Azure Pricing Calculator is your go-to web-based tool for estimating Azure costs before you commit."

**Guidelines:**
- One callback per episode is usually enough
- Place it early, in the foundation section, to orient returning viewers
- Phrase it as a natural conversation reference, not a formal citation
- Don't assume viewers watched prior episodes — the callback should also work as a brief standalone statement

---

## Series context in the outline

When producing an outline for a series episode, note at the top:
- Series name
- This episode's position (e.g., "Episode 2 of 4")
- What prior episodes covered (brief summary)
- What later episodes will cover (if known)

This ensures callbacks and forward references land correctly.
