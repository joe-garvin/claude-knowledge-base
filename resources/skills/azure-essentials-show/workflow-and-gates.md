# Workflow and gates

The production process has three stages with hard gates between them. The gates are non-negotiable.

---

## Stage 1: Outline

**Trigger:** Source materials and kickoff notes are available.

**Purpose:** Capture the full substance of the episode in structured form before any dialogue is written. The outline is a standalone deliverable — it goes to SMEs for review and approval before scripting begins.

**What to produce:**
- Comprehensive hierarchical outline (Roman numerals, letters, sub-bullets)
- Speaker assignments for every section: `[Thomas]` or `[Guest Name]`
- Complete talking points with substance — not topic labels
- Visual aid placeholders with specificity where known
- CTAs with URLs where known
- 2–3 refined title options
- Source material citations at the end

**Length:** Outlines may approach script length in word count. That is expected. A comprehensive outline makes Stage 2 a translation exercise, not a content development phase.

**Gate:** Deliver the outline and title options. Stop. Do not proceed to script until SME feedback has been received and incorporated.

---

## Stage 2: Bullet-point script

**Trigger:** SME feedback on the outline has been received and the outline has been revised and approved.

**Purpose:** Translate the approved outline into conversational dialogue.

**What to produce:**
- Speaker name in brackets, followed by bullet points
- Never numbered lists in dialogue sections
- Visual aid callouts carried forward from the outline, placed inline
- Natural conversation rhythm — see `length-and-pacing.md`

**Gate:** Script is complete when it matches the approved outline. Do not introduce new content at this stage without flagging it.

---

## Stage 3: Revisions

**Trigger:** SME or host feedback on the script (individual comments or batched).

**Approach:**
- Revise only the specific section identified in each comment
- Preserve all other sections unchanged
- Maintain consistent tone and formatting with surrounding content
- If a revision in one section creates a conflict elsewhere, flag it before proceeding

**Common revision types:**
- Technical accuracy (language precision, terminology)
- Emphasis (shifting weight between points)
- Demo scope (swapping or expanding demo content)
- Language (naturalness, corporate-speak removal)

---

## Behavior guardrails

- Always confirm understanding of source materials before starting the outline
- Never produce a script from source materials alone — the outline gate always applies
- When source materials are unclear or incomplete, flag the ambiguity explicitly; do not resolve it silently
- When demo scope is uncertain, include a placeholder with specific questions rather than inventing details
- When revising, make only the requested change — do not introduce unrelated improvements
- If a revision affects other sections, flag the dependency before proceeding
