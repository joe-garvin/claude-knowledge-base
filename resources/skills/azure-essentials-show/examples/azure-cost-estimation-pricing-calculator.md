II. Intro and overview of pricing calculator [Britt]
a. What the pricing calculator is and what it does
   i. The Azure Pricing Calculator is a web-based tool that's the gold standard for estimating Azure costs and making informed decisions about your cloud spending—whether you're a small business owner or an enterprise-level organization.
   ii. It's designed to help you simulate service configuration and estimated usage in order to deliver realistic pricing scenarios.
b. Why you should use the pricing calculator – three ways it empowers you
   i. First, you get precision in your estimates through service-specific inputs. It uses real-time pricing data across all global regions and comes with pre-built templates for common workloads. For enterprise customers, it can show you any consumption discounts you get with your enterprise agreement.
   ii. Second, it brings clarity to your decision-making. You can model different scenarios side by side and compare various architectures to see the cost impact of your design choices.
   iii. And third, it helps you deploy with confidence through clear cost breakdowns. Every service, every SKU is itemized, and you can export comprehensive reports for stakeholder reviews or compliance audits.
c. How to get to the pricing calculator
   i. There are two main ways: go directly to azure.microsoft.com/pricing/calculator, or find the pricing calculator link under the Pricing section in the main navigation menu.

III. Strategic approaches to using the calculator [Britt]
a. Collaborative and iterative mindset
   i. Pricing isn't a one-time checkbox exercise—it's an ongoing, collaborative conversation that evolves with your project.
   ii. Think of it as three iterative steps: understand your solution architecture, configure those services with realistic specifications, and estimate your usage patterns.
b. Who uses the calculator
   i. Engineering teams and FinOps professionals on the technical side; IT decision makers, procurement teams, and finance teams on the business side.
   ii. The same estimate can serve both audiences.
c. When teams use the calculator
   i. Pre-purchase for forecasting and validating architectures. Post-deployment to review and optimize actual spending against original estimates.

   i. Three main sections: the product picker at the top, the estimate and product configuration section (like a shopping cart), and the estimation summary at the bottom.
   ii. Logged-in users see personalized pricing based on enterprise agreements or negotiated discounts.

V. CTA [Britt]
a. Try the pricing calculator today with a real project. Configure a service and see how estimates change as you adjust parameters.
b. Find comprehensive tutorials and best practices on Microsoft Learn.
