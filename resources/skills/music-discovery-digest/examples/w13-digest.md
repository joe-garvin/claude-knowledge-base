## New discoveries

**Fabiano — Landmarks**
Label: E35 / Basic Channel · Genre: ambient dub, concrète
A pre-Mammo ambient suite from the Amsterdam producer behind the Short Span LP Boomkat has been pushing. This is the earlier, longer-form record on Basic Channel's E35 sublabel — dub-concrète, flotation tank material, over an hour of shapeshifting tones. Basic Channel is a direct ancestor of things you already love: Shinichi Atobe, Vainqueur, dub techno generally. Fabiano doesn't appear in your library or playlists — this is a clean discovery.
Boomkat: https://boomkat.com/products/landmarks-d6a06a7a-3bb2-424d-8f36-d0ead3ade84e
Preview: https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Fabiano+Landmarks+E35+Basic+Channel

## New releases from artists you know

**Passarani — Analog Fingerprints Vol. 0** *(new this week)*
Label: Numbers · Genre: acid, electro, Detroit techno · Released: March 27, 2026
Archival comp of his early 2000s Roman productions on Plasmek and Pigna — the records that originally hooked the Numbers founders as teenagers at Rubadub. Detroit techno rooted, Chicago house inflected, with Autechre-adjacent IDM experimentation. Numbers is a core label in your playlists and Passarani appears there twice. The archival framing makes this more discovery than known quantity.
Boomkat: https://boomkat.com/products/analog-fingerprints-vol-0
Preview: https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Passarani+Analog+Fingerprints+Vol+0+Numbers

**Loidis — One Day** *(recent release, may be new to you)*
Label: Incienso · Genre: deep house, dub techno
Brian Leeds (Huerco S.) in his deepest house and techno mode — hour-long, micro-dosed, dub-wise. Boomkat names Shinichi Atobe, DJ Sprinkles, and Vainqueur as reference points. Loidis appears in your Liked Songs (2 tracks), so you may already be on this. If you haven't heard the full album, worth the time.
Boomkat: https://boomkat.com/products/one-day-50e8918f-7db8-4c16-a0bf-eac13728ca1d
Preview: https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Loidis+One+Day+Incienso+Huerco+S

**Low Jack — Market** *(new 7")*
Label: Bambe · Genre: mutant house, electro
Low Jack is in your Liked Songs. Single 7" on Bambounou's Bambe label — heavy mutant house with Bambounou electro remix. Short form but a useful temperature check on where he's at.
Boomkat: https://boomkat.com/products/market-3073fd71-113c-4223-a110-030539b67e6f
Preview: https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Low+Jack+Market+Bambe+Bambounou

## Wildcard

**Genioh Yamashirogumi — Ecophony Rinne (1986, first wax reissue)**
Label: Time Capsule · Genre: orchestral, global, electronic
Pre-Akira work from the Symphonic Suite Akira collective. 200+ musicians, Tibetan horns, Bulgarian chorales, gamelan, central African field recordings. Not electronic, not house — but the scope and the crate-digger curiosity are completely aligned with what feeds your Madlib obsession. A serious artifact getting its first vinyl reissue in 40 years.
Boomkat: https://boomkat.com/products/ecophony-rinne
Preview: https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Genioh+Yamashirogumi+Ecophony+Rinne

## Also noted

- **Kjell Bjørgeengen & Lasse Marhaug — Flood Coil** (Smalltown Supersound) — harsh noise × outsider techno, Pan Sonic lineage. Interesting if you want to go harder.
- **Mika Vainio — Metri** (Sähkö, back in stock) — debut '94 minimal techno masterpiece. Pan Sonic / snd lineage. If this isn't already in your collection it's a genuine gap.
- **Paul St. Hilaire — Tikiman Vol. 1** (Kynant) — Rhythm & Sound vocalist retrospective. Peripheral to your taste but the Berlin dub connection is real.
