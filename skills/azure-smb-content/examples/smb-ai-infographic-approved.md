# Example: SMB AI use cases infographic — final approved copy

**Source:** Microsoft SMB AI Fundamentals Infographic, approved March 2026
**Client:** Christina Tsai, Microsoft SMB Integrated Marketing
**Designer:** Joy Baer

**What this example demonstrates:**
- The four-beat use case structure (business problem → what changed → business outcome → inclusive closer) in finished, approved form
- How Azure/Microsoft is mentioned naturally once per use case without feeling like a checkbox
- How the inclusive closer varies in syntax and emphasis across all four use cases
- How the proof point opener ("For one [descriptor]..." / "One [descriptor]...") is varied across sections
- The overall intro pattern: two sentences, outcome-first, no AI fundamentals explanation

**Notable decisions:**
- Intro avoids explaining what AI is — jumps directly to "businesses are doing this now"
- All four customer companies are named (Gojob, Assembly Software, CallRevu, Shift Technology) — these were confirmed as approved for external use
- The CTA landed on Azure Accelerate (tertiary option per the CTA hierarchy), not Azure AI Foundry — confirmed with Christina
- "Putting AI to work" appears in both headline and intro body; flagged during drafting but carried through in final

## Headline

Real Businesses. Real Results. Powered by Azure AI

## Intro

Businesses of every size are saving time, serving customers better, and making smarter decisions with AI, in ways that are more practical and accessible than you might expect. Here are four ways businesses are putting AI to work and seeing measurable impact right now.

---

## Chat with your data

**Business problem**

Every growing business runs on knowledge, and it’s usually scattered in various places: buried in email threads, saved across shared drives, or stored in someone’s head. When your team needs an answer, it can often take more time hunting for the right answer instead of acting on it. And when someone leaves the company, they take that critical knowledge with them.

**What changed**

AI connects to the documents and systems a business already has and lets anyone on the team ask questions in plain language. AI generates accurate, sourced answers in seconds, without needing to hunt through folders or drives. And Microsoft Azure keeps your knowledge secure, so your business data stays private and accessible only to your team.

**Business outcome**

Gojob is a staffing platform that matches temporary workers with employers. Using Azure OpenAI Service, they built a recruiter tool that lets anyone on the team search a database of two million candidate profiles using plain language questions, the same way you’d ask a colleague. Instead of manually sorting through hundreds of potential matches, recruiters receive a short list of the most relevant candidates in minutes. Their placement rate rose from 60% to 95%, and the number of candidates contacted per vacancy dropped from as many as 80 down to fewer than two.

**Inclusive closer**

The starting point for using AI is simpler than most businesses expect. AI works with the information you already have and makes it findable, without needing a dedicated IT project or major data overhaul.

---

## Document intelligence

**Business problem**

Most growing businesses juggle a never-ending stream of documentation. You may have a collection of invoices, contracts, intake forms, and inspection reports, and each needs to be processed to keep your business moving. It can be time-consuming work, and when done by humans, it is prone to errors and difficult to scale.

**What changed**

AI reads documents and automatically extracts the most relevant information. This allows you to automate invoice reconciliation, contract renewal flags, and process intake forms — all without anyone having to do it by hand. And Microsoft Azure handles the extraction securely, so the focus stays on data exceptions rather than data entry.

**Business outcome**

One legal software platform turned a multi-day paralegal review process into something that happens automatically when files arrive. Assembly Software built NeosAI on Azure AI Foundry to automate document-heavy workflows, such as reading incoming files, extracting key information, and populating case records. Law firms using NeosAI saved up to 25 hours per case on manual data entry and document review, and cut document drafting time from 40 hours to just minutes.

**Inclusive closer**

For any business managing a constant stream of documents, AI can help you recover this precious time. AI works with documents exactly as they arrive, without requiring any changes to how those documents are created or sent.

---

## Customer service

**Business problem**

Customers expect fast, helpful responses, but most growing businesses can’t staff around the clock. AI handles routine customer questions automatically, any time of day, so customers receive faster answers. This frees your team to focus on complex inquiries, sensitive situations, and relationships that help build your business. This is a way that AI works alongside your existing support process, and Microsoft Azure provides the security and reliability to run it at scale.

**Business outcome**

CallRevu is an auto dealership platform that helps dealerships analyze customer calls and automate follow-up. They began implementing Azure AI to monitor and score every incoming call; the system flagged missed opportunities and triggered automated follow-up so nothing fell through the cracks. Dealership staff went from spending hours manually reviewing call logs to focusing their time on live customer conversations. Across CallRevu’s network, that translated to a 15% lift in lead conversion, 20% fewer missed opportunities, and 10% higher customer satisfaction.

**Inclusive closer**

Better customer service doesn’t always mean more staff. With AI handling the volume, customers can get answers when it’s convenient for them. That translates to faster responses, fewer dropped opportunities, and a team that’s free to focus on higher-impact work.

---

## Security and fraud detection

**Business problem**

Fraud gets faster and more sophisticated every day, which can make it a challenge for investigators to manually review the sheer volume of transactions, claims, and account activity that flows through a growing business.

**What changed**

AI monitors that activity continuously, flagging suspicious patterns in real time so teams can investigate immediately. This scales the amount of transactions that investigators can cover, while freeing them to focus on cases that require human judgment. By employing Microsoft Azure in this process, businesses keep their data secure and private, with the reliability to handle operations of any size.

**Business outcome**

Shift Technology helps hundreds of insurers detect claims fraud. They use Azure Document Intelligence and Azure Vision in Foundry Tools to automatically read, classify, and extract information from documents that previously required weeks of manual sorting and labeling. That cut classification from weeks to days, and helped investigators focus on cases that require human judgment. With more than 2.6 billion policies and claims analyzed to date, insurers can flag suspicious activity faster and catch the kind of fraud that manual review would usually miss.

**Inclusive closer**

For most businesses, fraud prevention used to involve sampling. Now, AI gives investigators the ability to scale coverage and review everything, regardless of the size of their team.
